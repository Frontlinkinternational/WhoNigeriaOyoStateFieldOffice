The World Health Organization (WHO) supports Oyo State in strengthening its health system and improving public health outcomes. Its work helps to promote health, keep the world safe and serve the vulnerables as highlighted below:

1.. Immunization and Polio Eradication
WHO in Collaboration with Oyo State Government Coordinates the Global Polio Eradication Initiative in Oyo State
2. Disease Prevention and Control
Supports vaccination campaigns (e.g., polio, measles, COVID-19).
Helps Oyo State to respond to disease outbreaks (e.g., Vaccines Preventable Diseases like: cholera, Lassa fever, yellow fever). Works on providing technical supports to malaria control, HIV/AIDS, and tuberculosis programs.
2. Emergency Preparedness and Response
Assists in managing health emergencies, especially in conflict-affected areas (like the northeast). Coordinates health support during floods, disease outbreaks, and humanitarian crises.
3. Health System Strengthening
Provides technical support to improve primary healthcare delivery. Works with Oyoâ€™s Ministry of Health to develop policies, strategies, and training.
Supports data collection and use of health information systems.
4. Maternal and Child Health
Promotes safe childbirth, newborn care, and access to essential services for mothers and children.
Supports nutrition and growth monitoring. 
Helped Oyo and Nigeria eliminate wild poliovirus in 2020.
6. Health Promotion and Education
Encourages healthy behaviors through public education, especially on hygiene, nutrition, and disease prevention.
7. Partnership and Coordination
Collaborates with the State Ministry of Health, local authorities, NGOs, and other UN agencies.
Mobilizes resources and coordinates donor support for health programs.
